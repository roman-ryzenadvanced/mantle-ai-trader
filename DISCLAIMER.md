## 🤖 AI & AUTOMATION RISKS

### Limitations of AI Trading Systems

**Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning models have significant limitations:**

1. **Model Limitations**: AI models are trained on historical data and may not adapt well to unprecedented market conditions, black swan events, or regime changes.

2. **Technical Failures**: Software bugs, API failures, network issues, or infrastructure problems can cause incorrect signals, failed orders, or complete system failure.

3. **Data Quality**: The quality of AI predictions depends entirely on the quality of input data. Garbage in, garbage out.

4. **Overfitting**: Trading strategies may be overfitted to historical data and fail in live market conditions.

5. **Latency Issues**: Real-time trading requires low latency connections. Delays can result in significant slippage and losses.

6. **Model Drift**: AI models may degrade over time as market conditions change, requiring constant retraining and validation.

## 📊 BACKTESTING DISCLAIMER

### Historical Performance Limitations

**Backtesting results are HYPOTHETICAL and have inherent limitations:**

- **Hindsight Bias**: Backtests benefit from historical knowledge not available at the time of actual trading.
- **Survivorship Bias**: Historical data often excludes failed projects and delisted assets.
- **Look-Ahead Bias**: Backtests may inadvertently use future information.
- **Execution Assumptions**: Backtests assume perfect execution at historical prices, which is unrealistic.
- **Transaction Costs**: Backtests may not accurately account for fees, slippage, and market impact.
- **Market Conditions**: Historical market conditions may differ significantly from current or future conditions.

**HYPOTHETICAL PERFORMANCE RESULTS HAVE MANY INHERENT LIMITATIONS. NO REPRESENTATION IS BEING MADE THAT ANY ACCOUNT WILL OR IS LIKELY TO ACHIEVE PROFITS OR LOSSES SIMILAR TO THOSE SHOWN.**
